Nissan GTR R34






Pure, Top-Notch, Supercar

<=> It was featured prominently in the popular racing video game series, Gran Turismo.

<=> The R34 Skyline GT-R was introduced in 1998, and was available from 1998 to 2002.

<=> In 2002, Nissan ceased production of the GT-R R34, and it was succeeded by the GT-R R35 in 2007.

<=> The Nissan GT-R R34 is a sports car produced by the Japanese automaker Nissan from 1999 to 2002.

<=> It is part of the Nissan Skyline GT-R series and is known for its powerful engine and advanced all-wheel-drive system.

<=> The shorter wheelbase and more streamlined body of the R34 helped it to achieve even higher performance than its predecessors.

<=> The GT-R R34 is powered by a twin-turbocharged 2.6-liter inline-six engine, which produces 276 horsepower and 293 lb-ft of torque.

<=> A technologically advanced display unit set the model apart, while it's RB26DETT twin-turbo I6 engine produced impressive horsepower.

<=> The GT-R R34 is also famous for its performance in motorsports, particularly in the Japanese touring car championship and in the popular street racing scene.

<=> However, the GT-R R34 remains a highly sought-after sports car among enthusiasts, and its iconic design and performance continue to be celebrated in car culture today.
